Signal-free coordination for autonomous traffic (sixth-generation cellular, vehicle-to-vehicle) International Journal of Advanced Computer Science and Applications · sixth-generation wireless · vehicle-to-vehicle · signal-free intersections · learning-based control

Novel contribution. Reinforcement-learning–oriented vehicle-to-vehicle framing for signal-free intersections, with sixth-generation wireless connectivity assumptions tied to control policies aimed at throughput, safety, and emissions.

Impact

  • Presented to NASA on how the approach relates to latency and real-time coordination constraints in environments where they already face strict timing and reliability requirements.
  • Stakeholder interest from the Port of Oakland to explore applications relevant to port-adjacent congestion and heavy vehicle flow.
  • Collaboration with Amtrak to explore how coordination concepts may address rail–road and multimodal interface coordination challenges.

Decentralized multi-agent reinforcement learning for scheduling arXiv preprint · decentralized partially observable Markov decision process · cluster traces · decentralized scheduling

Novel contribution. Decentralized partially observable Markov decision process formulation with a lightweight actor–critic architecture evaluated on trace-derived workloads (e.g., public cluster traces), emphasizing reproducibility and edge-deployable dependencies.

Impact

  • Demonstrates measurable scheduling gains versus strong baselines on published experimental protocols (see preprint).
  • Releases code and data paths to support independent verification—relevant for systems research credibility.
  • Applicable to cloud, edge, and cluster environments where decentralized control reduces single points of failure.

Novel contribution. Deep deterministic policy gradient formulation for semantic-aware resource allocation in sixth-generation slicing scenarios, enhanced using a generative adversarial network.

Impact

  • Targets operator-scale fifth-generation and sixth-generation wireless and edge deployments where quality-of-service classes must track application semantics, not only raw bitrate.
  • Connects machine learning control policies to spectrum efficiency objectives relevant to future network planning.
