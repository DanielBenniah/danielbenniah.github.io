Decentralized multi-agent reinforcement learning for scheduling Under review · IEEE Access · arXiv preprint · decentralized scheduling

Novel contribution. Decentralized partially observable Markov decision process formulation with a lightweight actor–critic architecture evaluated on trace-derived workloads (e.g., public cluster traces), emphasizing reproducibility and edge-deployable dependencies.

Impact

  • Demonstrates measurable scheduling gains versus strong baselines on published experimental protocols (see preprint).
  • Releases code and data paths to support independent verification—relevant for systems research credibility.
  • Applicable to cloud, edge, and cluster environments where decentralized control reduces single points of failure.
